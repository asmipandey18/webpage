Personal Life

Virat kohli's personal life is marked by his high-profile marriage to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma. The two began dating in 2013,and after a few years of being in the public eye together,they got married in a private caremony in December 2017 in italy.The couple has one daughter, vamika and one son Akaay.

Kohli comes from a close-knit Punjabi family.His father prem kohli,was a criminal lawyer,and his Mother,Soraj Kohli,served as a housewife. He has an older borther,vikas and an older sister,Bhawna.His father passed away in 2006 a loss that deeply affected kohli and played a pivotal role in shaping his approach to life and cricket.Outside of cricket,kohli is known for his passion for fitness and healthy living.He follows a strict diet and exercise routine and has been a vocal advocate for a plant-based lifestyle.He has ventured into business,co-owning the indian super league(ISL) football club FC Goa and having stakes in various other business ventures. Kohli is also actively involved in philanthrophy through the Virat kohli foundation which focuses on supporting underprivileged children and young athletes in india. Despite his fame,he remains grounded and often shares glimpses of his family life and personal moments on social media.


Kohli's personality off the field is a mix of confidence,humility,and a deep commitment to his family and causes he belives in,making him not just a cricketing icon but also a popular figure in indian culture.Virat kohli is one of the most-followed athletes on social media,with millions of followes across instagram,Twitter,and Facebook. He has leveraged these platforms not only update fans about his views on various issues ranging from fitness to politics.

Kohli has acknowledged a belief in superstitions and owns various lucky charms and rituals that he feels serve to bring him good fourtune on the cricket field. One such tradition involves the donning of black Wristband. Additionally,he prefers a particulars pair of gloves,which he has consistently worn. Furthermore, kohli has been observed sporting a kara, a traditional bangle often worn for religious or spiritual purposes, on his right arms since 2012. In addition to the Previously mentioned superstitions, kohli has also established the ritual of consistently donning white shoes on the field. kohli has tattos of the Hindu deity LORD SHIVA, the sacred syllable "OM",the names of his parents, prem and saroj,a tribal emblem, a representation of a serene monastery, a samural warrior, the astrological symbol of the Scorpion, and his ODI and Test match cap numbers.
